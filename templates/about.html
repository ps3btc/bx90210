in early 2010, i decided to visit twitter.com to see if anyone out there was tweeting their experiences with cancer. to my surprise i found plenty of people doing so in real time. i also found hoards of spam, links and other crud. i felt the need to curate these results automatically. and so i decided to build tweetandbeat.com a "vertical" index (in a sense) that sits on top of twitter only to address the cancer related tweets. the following were my reasons for building the service:

the first: was to get people (like you and me, who are browsing the website) to offer a few words of encouragement when someone tweets something like this:

My family has been fighting cancer for 3 years now. Have lost so far 2 out of 5 with it. I am also a widow from heart disease. (tweet)

or to tell them (a perfect stranger) that you are happy for them when they score a victory such as this:

Longest 10 days of my life. Cancer-free is all that matters today! Bad mammogram...breathing HUGEST sigh of relief. Can now get brain back! (tweet)

each one of us has so much empathy and so much love to give strangers. so why not give this love to ones who are suffering from the dreaded disease? technology lets you do this in real time just when they could use (your) kind words of encouragement. when you find a tweet you want to reply to, simply press and offer your words of encouragement.

the second: was to get people with the disease, to discover others with the disease in real time. the service stores cancer related tweets going back to early jan, 2010. go on, hit next and browse.

and finally: was to remove spam and build a social network on top of these cancer related tweets. you can favorite these tweets, and in general curate and personalize the experience just for yourself. i'm still building this service.

disclaimer: this service will always be a work in process. i sincerly hope you find this service useful. you can contact me anytime by sending an email to hareesh.nagarajan@gmail.com. i have horrible user interface skills, so if you think you can redesign this website i'd love to hear from you.
